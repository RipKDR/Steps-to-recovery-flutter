# "Build Your Rat Park" — Life Environment Feature
*Feature concept — 2026-03-22 — Status: Brainstorming*

---

## The Concept

A visual, interactive representation of the user's life environment — their Rat Park. Not a checklist. Not a dashboard. Something closer to a living map of what makes their life worth staying in.

The name is internal/conceptual. The user-facing name could be "Your World", "Life Map", "What Keeps You Here", or something even simpler.

---

## Why This Exists

If the product philosophy is "build a richer environment," the user should be able to *see* their environment and watch it grow. Abstinence counts measure what you're avoiding. This measures what you're building.

It also gives the AI sponsor a richer understanding of the user's life beyond app usage data. What do they care about? What have they built? What's still empty?

---

## What Gets Tracked

The Rat Park has five dimensions — each a spoke on a visual wheel, or a section of a growing map:

### 1. Connection
Human relationships — their quality and frequency, not just their existence.
- Sponsor / accountability partner (in-app)
- Meetings attended
- Contact hub activity (who they've reached out to)
- Family relationship quality (self-reported)
- Friendships (self-reported, prompted by sponsor)

### 2. Meaning & Purpose
What gives their days direction.
- Step work progress (if 12-step)
- Goals set and tracked
- Things they're learning or working toward
- Service or helping others (AA calls this the 12th step — it works)

### 3. Structure & Routine
The cage needs walls that feel safe, not confining.
- Daily check-in consistency
- Sleep patterns (from Health integration if enabled)
- Eating, exercise (self-reported or Health integration)
- Employment / study status (optional, self-reported)

### 4. Wellbeing
How they're actually doing.
- Mood trend (from check-ins)
- Craving trend (from check-ins)
- Stress level (self-reported)
- Physical health basics

### 5. Growth
The sense that they're becoming someone.
- EQ development (tracked internally, surfaced as observations not scores)
- Skills being built
- Milestones hit
- Things the AI sponsor has noticed changing in them

---

## Visual Treatment

Not a clinical radar chart. Something warmer.

**Option A — The Park:**
A literal park illustration that grows over time. Empty benches become occupied. A bare tree gets leaves. A path appears. Connected people appear as warm lights. Simple, metaphorical, satisfying.

**Option B — The Web:**
Concentric rings with five spokes. Inner ring = sparse. Outer ring = rich. Colour-coded. Beautiful when full. Obviously incomplete when sparse. Each section tappable for detail.

**Option C — The House:**
Building a home. An empty lot on day one. Foundation, walls, windows, garden, lights. Each Rat Park dimension = a part of the house. Satisfying to build. Obvious what's missing.

**My instinct:** Option A (the park) best fits the brand metaphor and is emotionally resonant without being clinical.

---

## AI Sponsor Integration

The sponsor actively references the Rat Park:
- *"Your Connection spoke has been sparse lately. Who have you talked to this week — not me, a real person?"*
- *"Your Growth section lit up this week. What's different?"*
- *"The part of your Park I'm most concerned about right now is structure. You've missed your morning check-in 4 days running."*

The Rat Park also generates sponsor conversation prompts. If the Meaning spoke is empty for 3 weeks, the sponsor asks: *"What gave your days direction before? What did you used to care about?"*

---

## User Control

- Users choose how much to fill in. Nothing is mandatory.
- Each dimension has both automatic (from app usage data) and manual (self-report) inputs
- Sensitivity levels: some dimensions visible only to the user, some shareable (milestone posts draw from this)
- The user can name their own Rat Park. Some users will love the metaphor. Others will want to call it "My Life" or "Home Base."

---

## Gamification Layer (Light Touch)

Not badges and points. Something more subtle:
- The park/web/house genuinely looks better as it fills in
- Sponsor notices and comments on growth
- Milestone celebrations reference specific Rat Park wins: *"90 days clean. And look at your Connection section — you've built something real there."*
- Community posts can reference dimensions: *"My Meaning section felt empty for months. Here's what changed it."*
