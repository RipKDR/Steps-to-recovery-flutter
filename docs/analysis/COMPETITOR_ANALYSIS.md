## Executive Summary

The recovery app market is fragmented between **passive trackers** (I Am Sober), **clinical programs** (Quit Genius), and **reference tools** (AA Big Book). No competitor owns the **proactive recovery companion** space—an app that intervenes before relapse rather than just counting days.

**Our Position:** Steps to Recovery = The first proactive recovery operating system with a living AI sponsor.

2. **Programs** (Quit Genius)

## Competitive Threats

### High Threat
1. **I Am Sober adds AI** - They have the users, we'd lose differentiation
2. **Apple/Google build recovery tools** - Platform-level competition

### Medium Threat
1. **Quit Genius goes B2C** - Clinical credibility + AI
2. **New entrant with similar approach** - First-mover advantage temporary

### Low Threat
1. **AA launches official app** - Unlikely, conservative organization
2. **Nomo modernizes** - Dated tech, slow to change

### Mitigation Strategies
- Ship fast, establish first-mover advantage in "AI sponsor" space
- Build deep moat through 3-tier memory (not easily copyable)
- Focus on privacy (harder for big companies to match)
- Establish 12-step authenticity (hard to fake)

## Key Takeaways

2. **Privacy is our moat** - competitors are cloud-first
