# Product: Steps to Recovery

## One-Line Description

A privacy-first Rat Park builder — not a sobriety tracker — for anyone whose cage got too small.

## Problem Statement

Existing recovery apps (I Am Sober, Sober Grid, etc.) are relapse counters built around shame and streaks. They miss the actual mechanism of recovery: the 12-step program works because it accidentally creates **Rat Park conditions** — community, routine, meaning, service.

Bruce Alexander's Rat Park experiment showed addiction is a response to environment, not a moral failing. Johann Hari: "The opposite of addiction is connection." Gabor Maté: "Don't ask why the addiction — ask why the pain."

## Solution

A companion app that actively builds connection, meaning, and behavioral scaffolding. Not just a log — a living system that notices patterns, nudges toward Rat Park conditions, and provides a trusted AI presence modeled on the best sponsor imaginable.

## Target Users

**Primary:** Anyone in or adjacent to a 12-step program (AA, NA, CA, Al-Anon)
**Secondary:** People in recovery who don't identify as addicts — behavioral addictions, harm reduction, sobriety-curious
**Not:** Clinical professionals, court-mandated users (no tracking/reporting features)

## Competitive Moat

1. **Memory** — Sponsor knows who you are over time. No competitor has this.
2. **Privacy** — AES-256 before any server ever sees data. No competitor does this.
3. **Proactiveness** — App reaches out. Competitors are passive logs.
4. **Rat Park philosophy** — Building toward connection, not away from substances.

## Non-Features (Explicit Exclusions)

- No reporting/tracking to third parties
- No abstinence requirement (harm reduction compatible)
- No social media integration
- No gamification that trivializes the recovery journey
- No ads, ever
